Bean and Halloumi Stew

Description

A quick, easy midweek supper that's meatless but easily enjoyed even by the omnivores. The stew is hearty and filling while the halloumi adds a delicious tang that contrasts the richness of the stew.

This dish must be served with some crusty bread--or, even better, garlic bread--to sop up the remnants of the stew.

Ingredients

  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 red pepper, thinly sliced
  • 2 garlic cloves, crushed
  • 3 tbsp red chilli pesto, sundried tomato pesto or vegan alternative
  • 1 heaped tsp ground coriander
  • 400g can mixed beans, drained and rinsed
  • 400g can chopped tomatoes
  • 1/2 x 250g block halloumi, sliced
  • 1/2 small bunch of coriander, finely chopped

Steps

  1. Heat 2 tbsp of oil in a frying pan over medium heat. Add the onion and pepper, along with a pinch of salt and fry for 10 minutes or until softened
  2. Add the garlic, pesto and ground coriander, and cook for 1 minute
  3. Tip in the beans and tomatoes along with 1/2 can of water, then bring to a simmer and cook uncovered for 10 minutes
  4. Add the remaining oil to a separate frying pan over medium heat. Fry the halloumi for 2 minutes on each side or until golden brown
  5. Taste the beans for seasoning, then spoon into deep bowls. Top with the halloumi and scatter over the chopped coriander
