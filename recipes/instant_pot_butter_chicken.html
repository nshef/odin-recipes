Instant Pot Butter Chicken

Description

I was initially underwhelmed by my Instant Pot while I was trying to understand exactly what types of recipes it was useful for. However, this butter chicken completely changed my mind. It literally could not be easier, and makes restaurant-quality curry so good that this recipe alone got the creator a cookbook deal.

This must be served over rice with naan, no question.

Ingredients

Note: This recipe originally had American measurements (i.e., ounces and pounds), but I have converted them as accurately as possible to use UK ingredients.

  • 1 400g can tomatoes (I used chopped tomatoes to avoid blending on the last step; otherwise use whole)
  • 5-6 cloves garlic
  • 1-2 tsp minced ginger
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp kosher salt
  • 1 tsp garam masala, plus an additional 1 tsp for finishing
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 500g chicken thigh fillets
  • 115g unsalted butter, cut into cubes
  • 120ml double cream
  • 1/4-1/2 cup chopped coriander

Steps

  1. Place all the ingredits into an Instant Pot in the order listed except for the butter, cream, coriander and 1 tsp of the garam masala, mixing the sauce well before you place the chicken on top of it
  2. Close the cooker and set for 10 minutes on high, and let it release pressure naturally for 10 minutes. After that, release all remaining pressure
  3. Open the pot and remove the chicken and slice into bite-sized chunks
  4. Blend together all of the remaining ingredients, preferably using an immersion blender
  5. Let the sauce cool slightly, then add the butter, cream, coriander and garam masala and stir well until incorporated
  6. Add the chicken back in and heat through
  7. Serve over rice
